Osman Ahmed Osman

Othman was born on April 6, 1917, in the city of Ismailia, Egypt, to a poor family. When Othman reached his third year, his older brother Muhammad gave up his education and went to work to provide a stable income for the family. In his childhood, Othman often worked to provide extra income for the family. After completing high school, Othman joined the Faculty of Engineering, Cairo University, majoring in the Civil Engineering Department. His economic condition did not differ much in that period from his childhood, as he did not have any money to bear the university fees or to pay the housing rent. However, these two problems did not last long, so he obtained a scholarship from the university, lived with his older sister, and traveled with a bicycle that he collected himself. Othman graduated from the university in 1940 with a bachelor's degree in civil engineering. Othman returned to Ismailia to work for his uncle, an illiterate but experienced contractor, for 18 months

شركه المقاولين العرب

The Arab Contractors Company is an Egyptian government joint stock company, subject to the law of public sector bodies, owned by the Ministry of Housing, Utilities and Urban Communities, working in the field of construction and construction of buildings, bridges, roads, tunnels, airports, dams, restoration of monuments, electromechanical works, engineering consultancy, and infrastructure projects. It was established in 1955 by Othman Ahmed Othman as an Egyptian joint stock company under the name “The Engineering Company for Industries and General Contracting.” its name was changed to “The Arab Contractors Company” in 1964. The company has 12 branches inside Egypt, and 28 branches in Africa and Asia.
