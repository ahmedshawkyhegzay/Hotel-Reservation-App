king Khufu

Pharaoh Khufu, the second king of the Fourth Dynasty, who followed Pharaoh Snefru on the throne, and who is likely to be his father. It is well established that his mother was Queen Hetepheres, whose tomb is located in the Giza cemetery. He is credited with building the Great Pyramid on the Giza Plateau as a tomb for him, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. But many other aspects of his rule are not documented. He sent expeditions to Wadi al-Maghara in Sinai to bring turquoise. Where he found his name and a picture representing him as he fell on the head of a person with a pin. He has a single ivory statue found in Abydos, his name engraved on the throne. the length of the statue is five centimeters; It is now in the Egyptian Museum. Judgment according to the papyrus (Turin) about twenty-three years. During his reign, the Great Pyramid was built in Giza, and it was the highest stone building in the world until the construction of a cathedral in 1350, and its height reached 160 meters, but the Pyramid of Khufu is the largest man-made building so far.

the bigger pyramid

The Great Pyramid or the Pyramid of Khufu is the only surviving monument of the Seven Wonders of the World. It is located in the Giza pyramids area in Egypt, which is registered as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The construction of the pyramid dates back to about the year 2560 BC, when it was built as a tomb for the fourth dynasty pharaoh Khufu, and its construction continued for a period of 20 years. The construction of the Great Pyramid is a major cultural shift in the history of ancient Egypt. Khufu was influenced by his father, King Senefru, in building his pyramid. After his death, Khufu became the ruling god on earth, and it became necessary for him to think about building his tomb, which is the first national project in ancient Egypt, in which professional workers from all over Egypt participated in its construction. The Great Pyramid, with its original height of 148 meters, remained the tallest building completed by man on earth over a period of 3800 years..
